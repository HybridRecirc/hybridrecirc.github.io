What's This?

One of my 2025 New Year's resolutions was to finally have an updated portfolio outlining my projects accessible to friends and new acquaintances, as well as potential employers. The goal of this website is to showcase the work that I have done throughout my undergraduate degree.

Additionally, this website will also function as a living journal of the things that I am actively working on, with an emphasis on documentation and open sourcing my findings, heavily inspired by Nakka Rocketry.

This is a journal website presenting my thought processes in the form of project documentation, so expect the tone to be informal and lighthearted.

Undergrad

Collegiate Rocketry

  • Project Pegasus (IREC 2023)

    • My first major multi-disciplinary project. Pioneered a novel paraffin fuel blend that solved burn stability issues. UCF's first hybrid to fly. Chamber failure in flight. :/ - Read more...

  • Project Helios (IREC 2024)

    • Taking everything I learned from the previous year, Helios was set to break barriers beyond what any of us thought was achievable. Bordering on the P impulse class, hands down the most powerful rocket ever designed and tested by KXR and UCF. Nozzle failure during testing. - Read more...

  • Project SABR (Fall '24 - Spring '25)

    • My Senior Design project. Aiming to discover the operational limits of a Small-Scale H2/Air RDE combustor, and develop a ready to integrate solution for highly efficient propulsion needs. Success. - Read more...

Personal

  • Computed Tomography Image Processing

    • I developed MATLAB code that processes equally spaced X-ray exposures around an object and generates a 3D slice stack of the object's internal features. In order to get data to test and calibrate my program, I leveraged Blender to raytrace light going throug an .stl file and captured its scatter pattern on a surface. Read more...
