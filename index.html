What's This?

One of my 2025 New Year's resolutions was to finally have an updated portfolio outlining my projects accessible to friends and new acquaintances, as well as potential employers. The goal of this website is to showcase the work that I have done throughout my undergraduate degree.

Additionally, this website will also function as a living journal of the things that I am actively working on, with an emphasis on documentation and open sourcing my findings, heavily inspired by Nakka Rocketry.

This is an informal website presenting my thought processes in the form of project documentation, so expect the tone to be lighthearted. At the end of the day, it's just a personal website immitating geocities sites.

About Me!

Welcome to my website!

Please read this first!

I am an Aerospace Engineering student with a background in gas dynamics and propulsion, primarily through collegiate sounding rocketry. I have participated in the International Rocket Engineering Competition for a couple of years, primarily overseeing the development of the hybrid rocket motor for each participating rocket.

Currently, I am working on a Rotating Detonation Engine for my senior design project, with the intention of contributing to the understanding of RDEs, and creating an air breathing small-scale platform ready to be integrated with a vehicle.

I've dabbled in 2, 3, and 4 axis CNC machining, as well as resin (DLP) additive manufacturing, and have experience in design for manufacturing. Experienced using ProtoTRAK Mills and Lathes, as well as HAAS Vertical Machining Centers.

Beyond the purely engineering experience stuff, I like to spend my free time browsing for obsolete tech, playing guitar and reading. I like to collect physical music, mainly cassettes and MiniDisc. I love the 00s and 10s, hence this website's design.

I like listening to music, primarily prog rock and indie rock, but at the end of the day I love long format songs more than anything. I have a soft spot for pop, especially Chappell Roan.

I LOVE Car Seat Headrest.
